Adjusted Expected Value (Adj EV)

Bracket-aware EV calculation using fitted win probability model

1 The Problem

In a Calcutta auction, you're buying matchup pairs (e.g., buying the 1-seed also gives you their 16-seed opponent). But not all 1-seeds are equal—some are historically strong, others are weaker. Adj EV accounts for this using a bracket-aware model that calculates win probabilities against actual opponents, not just seed averages.

2 The Model

P(A beats B) = 1 / (1 + e-(α + β × log-odds-diff))
Logistic regression fitted to 16 years of tournament games

The model uses Barthag ratings converted to log-odds:
log_odds = log(barthag / (1 - barthag))

🎯 Fitted Model

Logistic regression trained on ~1000 tournament games (2008-2024). Coefficients validated against theoretical formula.

🏀 Bracket-Aware

Calculates exact win probabilities through each round using actual opponent Barthag ratings from the current bracket.

📈 Round-by-Round

P(win R64) × P(win R32 | R32 opponent) × P(win S16 | S16 opponents) × ...

⚖️ Result

EV = sum of P(reach round) × payout(round). Uses actual matchups, not historical averages.

3 How Bracket-Aware EV Works

Instead of using seed averages, we calculate through the actual bracket structure:

Round What We Calculate Example (Duke, East 1-seed)
R64 P(beat known 16-seed) P(Duke beats 16-seed) = 99.4%
R32 Weighted avg of P(beat 8-seed) and P(beat 9-seed) P(reach S16) = 99.4% × 95% = 94.4%
S16 Weighted avg across potential 4/5/12/13 seeds P(reach E8) = 94.4% × 78% = 73.6%
E8 Regional final vs opposite bracket half P(reach F4) = 73.6% × 65% = 47.8%
F4 National semi-final (avg strong opponent) P(reach Final) = 47.8% × 55% = 26.3%
CHAMP Championship game P(win title) = 26.3% × 55% = 14.5%

EV Calculation: EV = Σ P(reach round) × payout(round)
EV = 94.4%×1.5% + 73.6%×1.5% + 47.8%×5% + 26.3%×12% + 14.5%×20% = 10.76%

5 The Data Behind It

Model Training & Validation

  • Training data: ~1,000 tournament games (2008-2024, excluding 2020)
  • Barthag ratings from Barttorvik.com — a team's probability of beating an average D1 team on a neutral court
  • Logistic regression: Predicts P(team A wins) from log-odds difference of Barthag ratings
  • Theoretical validation: Fitted coefficient should be ~1.0 (if Barthag is well-calibrated)
  • Calibration check: Binned predictions match actual win rates (±5%)
✅
What it's good for: Capturing value from bracket structure—easy paths (weak nearby seeds) vs hard paths.
❌
What it can't do: Predict upsets or account for injuries, game-day factors, or "clutch" performance.

6 Key Takeaways

  • Bracket structure matters — a strong team in an easy region has higher EV than in a murderer's row
  • All 64 EVs sum to 100% — someone must win each dollar of the pot
  • Fitted model uses actual Barthag — not z-scores or seed averages
  • Use for relative comparisons — "Is Houston worth more than Duke?" rather than "Will Houston win?"
  • Combine with auction dynamics — a team with lower EV at a big discount can still be +EV

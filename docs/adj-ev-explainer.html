Adjusted Expected Value (Adj EV)

How we estimate what a team is really worth in a Calcutta auction

1 The Problem

In a Calcutta auction, you're buying matchup pairs (e.g., buying the 1-seed also gives you their 16-seed opponent). But not all 1-seeds are equal—some are historically strong, others are weaker. Adj EV accounts for this by adjusting the baseline expected value based on team quality.

2 The Formula

Adj EV = Base EV + (z-score × Δ)

📊 Base EV

Historical average payout for each matchup type (1/16, 2/15, etc.) based on 17 years of tournament data.

📏 Z-Score

How a team compares to others of the same seed. Calculated from Barthag (win probability vs. average team).

📈 Delta (Δ)

Percentage points of EV change per standard deviation. Derived from cross-validated regression model.

⚖️ Result

Strong-for-seed teams get boosted EV; weak-for-seed teams get reduced EV. Total still sums to 100%.

4 Example Calculations

Houston (1-seed, Midwest) — Strong for seed

Barthag: 0.9836 (highest among all 1-seeds)

z-score = +1.07 (1.07 std devs above 1-seed average)
Adj EV = 8.75% + (1.07 × 1.74%) = 10.61%

For a $20k pot: Base value $1,750 → Adj value $2,122

Florida (1-seed, West) — Weak for seed

Barthag: 0.9709 (lowest among all 1-seeds)

z-score = -1.32 (1.32 std devs below 1-seed average)
Adj EV = 8.75% + (-1.32 × 1.74%) = 6.45%

For a $20k pot: Base value $1,750 → Adj value $1,290

⚠️
Important caveat: In 2025, Florida (flagged as a "bad buy") won the championship! Adj EV identifies expected value, but single tournaments have massive variance. The model is probabilistic, not predictive.

5 The Data Behind It

Data Sources & Methodology

  • 17 years of NCAA tournament results (2008-2025, excluding 2020)
  • Barthag ratings from Barttorvik.com — a team's probability of beating an average D1 team on a neutral court
  • Leave-one-year-out cross-validation to prevent overfitting
  • R² = 0.47 for predicting tournament wins from seed + within-seed strength
  • Barthag correlation with tournament wins: r = 0.376 (strongest single predictor)
✅
What it's good for: Identifying relative value between teams of the same seed across regions.
❌
What it can't do: Predict upsets or account for matchup-specific factors (injuries, bracket position).

6 Key Takeaways

  • Z-scores sum to ~0 within each seed, so Adj EVs still total 100% of the pot
  • Bigger adjustments for lower seeds — being a strong 8-seed matters more than being a strong 1-seed
  • Use for relative comparisons — "Is Houston worth more than Duke?" rather than "Will Houston win?"
  • Combine with auction dynamics — a "bad" team at a discount can still be a good buy
